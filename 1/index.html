Part 3 — The Dolly Zoom

Create a dolly zoom by moving the camera while inversely changing focal length to keep the subject’s size constant and the background “breathing.”

Animation: dolly zoom (looped)
Capture notes (recommended)
  • Subject framed constant (e.g., shoulder-up) while you move.
  • Increase focal length as you step back (or decrease as you move forward).
  • Use markers on the ground for consistent steps; keep horizon steady.
  • Shoot stills (4–8+) or short video; stabilize if possible.

Reflection (2–4 sentences)

Why does the background stretch/Compress while the subject stays the same? Relate to perspective change from camera position vs. FOV cropping from focal length.

Discussion & Takeaways

  • Perspective is about position: moving the camera changes relative geometry; focal length changes FOV/cropping.
  • Portrait tip: back up, zoom in (~70–105mm equiv.) for natural proportions.
  • Architecture tip: choose position to control convergence and background scale.
  • Dolly zoom: hold subject size constant while changing position + focal length inversely.
